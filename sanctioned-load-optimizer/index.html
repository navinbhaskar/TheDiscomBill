Sanctioned Load Optimizer

Your fixed charge is billed on your sanctioned load every single month — used or not. Most households still carry the load set at connection time (or bumped for an AC long gone), and quietly overpay for years. Enter your load and your highest recorded demand — we run your DISCOM's real fixed-charge schedule and show the load you actually need, what it saves per year, and where cutting too far starts costing you penalties.

Where do I find my MD (maximum demand)?

Look for "MD", "Maximum Demand" or "Recorded Demand" on your bill — most DISCOMs print it near the meter readings, in kW or kVA. Smart meters record it automatically. Check your last 12 bills and take the highest value (your summer AC months will set it). No MD on your bills? Estimate the biggest load you switch on at once: a 1.5 ton inverter AC draws ≈ 1.5 kW, a geyser ≈ 2 kW, and lights/fans/fridge together ≈ 0.5 kW.

How this optimizer works

For every candidate load we price your bill with the same engine as our bill calculator — your DISCOM's fixed charge schedule (per-kW, tiered or flat), slab rates and duty — with your recorded demand held at your real MD. That's what makes the "too low" zone visible: below your MD, many tariffs bill an excess-demand penalty or re-base your load upward, which can wipe out the saving. The sweet spot keeps sensible headroom above your MD, and nothing more.

Load-reduction rules differ by DISCOM — most accept applications once per year, some charge a small processing fee, and new appliances (an extra AC, an EV charger) can push your MD back up.
