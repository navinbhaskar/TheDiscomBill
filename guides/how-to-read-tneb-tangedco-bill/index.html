How to Read Your TNEB / TANGEDCO Electricity Bill

5 min read · Updated 6 July 2026

Tamil Nadu bills are the most misread in India for one simple reason: TANGEDCO bills once every two months, so every slab, every free-unit limit and every comparison with other states must be done on a 2-month cycle. Add the 100 free units and the concession cliff at 500 units, and two similar households can see very different totals.

1. Bimonthly billing — the #1 source of confusion

Your "monthly" consumption is actually two months of units. When comparing with friends in other states — or using any calculator built on monthly slabs — halve your TNEB units first. Our Tamil Nadu calculator shows a monthly estimate; multiply by two to sanity-check a bimonthly bill.

2. The first 100 units are free — conditionally

Every domestic consumer gets the first 100 units of each 2-month cycle free. The catch: the full concession structure applies only if your cycle total stays within 500 units. Cross 500, and the concessional slab rates fall away — the whole bill is computed on the regular ladder. That's the "cliff" that makes a 510-unit cycle cost far more than a 490-unit one.

3. The slab ladder

Above the free block, slabs step at 200, 500 and 1000 units per cycle (telescopic), with the top slab applying beyond 1000. Current per-unit rates are on the TANGEDCO tariff page. The fixed charge for domestic connections is a modest flat amount per cycle, and electricity duty adds 5% on the energy charges.

4. Verify the total

  1. Confirm the reading dates span ~60 days — a longer cycle silently pushes units into higher slabs.
  2. Check units = (current − previous) × MF.
  3. Recompute: free 100 → slab ladder on the rest → duty. Or enter half your cycle units in the calculator and double the result.
  4. A jump with unchanged habits usually means the cycle crossed 500 units — see our bill-increase guide.

Frequently asked questions

Why is my TNEB bill for two months?
TANGEDCO bills domestic consumers bimonthly — meters are read every ~60 days and all slabs and free-unit limits are defined per 2-month cycle. Halve the units before comparing with monthly-billing states.
Are the first 100 units really free in Tamil Nadu?
Yes — the first 100 units of every 2-month cycle are free for domestic consumers. But the wider concessional structure applies only while the cycle total stays within 500 units; beyond that, regular slab rates apply to the whole consumption.
Why did my TNEB bill jump although my usage barely changed?
The most common cause is crossing the 500-unit threshold for the 2-month cycle, which removes the concessional rates for the entire bill — a 20-unit increase can add a disproportionate amount. Longer reading cycles and estimated readings are the next suspects.
Does TANGEDCO charge electricity duty?
Yes — Tamil Nadu adds electricity duty at 5% on the energy charges, one of the lower rates among large states. It appears as a separate line after the slab charges.
