How to Read Your TPCODL / Odisha Electricity Bill

6 min read · Updated 21 July 2026

Odisha is one of the simpler states to check an electricity bill in — one uniform OERC tariff applies across all four Tata Power distribution companies (TPCODL around Bhubaneswar–Cuttack, TPNODL in the north, TPWODL in the west and TPSODL in the south), and the domestic rates have not changed since April 2024. This guide decodes each line of the bill and shows how to recompute the total yourself.

One tariff, four DISCOMs

The Odisha Electricity Regulatory Commission (OERC) sets a single retail tariff for the whole state in its annual Retail Supply Tariff order. The FY 2026-27 order (24 March 2026) retained the existing rates for the third year running, so whichever TP company bills you, the schedule below applies. The current slab-by-slab detail for your DISCOM is on our Odisha tariff pages.

The energy charge — telescopic slabs

Domestic (LT) consumption is billed telescopically — each band's rate applies only to the units inside that band:

Monthly consumptionRate
First 50 units₹2.90/unit
51 – 200 units₹4.70/unit
201 – 400 units₹5.70/unit
Above 400 units₹6.10/unit

Example: 300 units = 50 × ₹2.90 + 150 × ₹4.70 + 100 × ₹5.70 = ₹1,420. These rates have been in force since 1 April 2024, when OERC cut every slab by 10 paise.

MMFC — the fixed charge per kW

The Monthly Minimum Fixed Charge for domestic connections is ₹20 for the first kW of connected load and ₹20 for each additional kW — effectively ₹20/kW/month. A typical 2 kW household pays ₹40. Commercial (General Purpose) connections pay ₹30/kW, with energy slabs of ₹5.90 / ₹7.00 / ₹7.60 at 100 and 300 units.

Kutir Jyoti — the flat ₹70 tariff

BPL households under the Kutir Jyoti scheme who stay within 30 units a month pay a single flat charge of ₹70/month — no energy charge, no per-kW MMFC. If consumption exceeds 30 units, the regular domestic schedule applies instead, which is why a small overshoot can change the bill's shape entirely.

Rebates most people miss

  • Prompt-payment rebate: 10 paise/unit for LT domestic and General Purpose consumers who pay by the due date.
  • Digital-payment rebate: 4% for LT domestic and single-phase GP consumers paying electronically — one of the most generous digital rebates in India.
  • Rural discount: a further 10 paise/unit for rural domestic consumers billed on actual meter reading.

On a ₹1,500 bill the digital rebate alone is worth about ₹60 a month — worth switching payment mode for.

Other lines on the bill

  • Electricity duty — a small state levy under the Odisha Electricity (Duty) Act, charged over and above the OERC tariff; for domestic connections it is a few paise per unit, far below the 5–16% seen in many states.
  • Meter reading & status — check the reading is marked actual, not estimated/provisional; estimated months are trued-up later and cause catch-up bills.
  • Arrears / DPS — outstanding amounts from earlier bills; pay the current month by the due date to keep the prompt-payment rebate.

Verify the total yourself

Enter your units and connected load in the Odisha bill calculator — it applies the telescopic slabs, the ₹20/kW MMFC and the Kutir Jyoti rule, and shows an itemised result to compare with the printed bill. If the printed total is more than a few rupees higher (before duty and arrears), check the slab arithmetic and whether the reading was estimated — our free Bill Review can take a look.

Frequently asked questions

What is the electricity rate per unit in Odisha in 2026?
Domestic rates are telescopic: ₹2.90/unit for the first 50 units, ₹4.70 for 51–200, ₹5.70 for 201–400 and ₹6.10 above 400 units per month, plus a ₹20/kW monthly fixed charge. The FY 2026-27 OERC order retained these rates, unchanged since April 2024.
Is the tariff the same for TPCODL, TPNODL, TPWODL and TPSODL?
Yes. OERC sets one uniform retail supply tariff for all four Tata Power DISCOMs in Odisha, so the slab rates, fixed charges and rebates are identical statewide — only the operating company on the bill differs.
What is Kutir Jyoti on an Odisha electricity bill?
Kutir Jyoti is the BPL tariff: households consuming up to 30 units a month pay only a flat ₹70 monthly charge, with no separate energy charge. Above 30 units, the normal domestic slab schedule applies.
What is MMFC on a TPCODL bill?
MMFC is the Monthly Minimum Fixed Charge — ₹20 for the first kW of connected load and ₹20 per additional kW for domestic connections (₹30/kW for commercial). It is payable regardless of consumption.
Did Odisha electricity rates increase in 2026?
No. The OERC Retail Supply Tariff order for FY 2026-27 (24 March 2026) retained the existing rates for the third consecutive year — domestic slabs have been unchanged since the 10-paise cut of April 2024.
How do I get the 4% rebate on my Odisha electricity bill?
Pay the bill digitally (app, portal, UPI or other electronic mode). OERC allows a 4% rebate for LT domestic and single-phase General Purpose consumers on digital payment, on top of the 10 paise/unit prompt-payment rebate for paying by the due date.
