How Much Does a 1.5 Ton AC Add to Your Electricity Bill?

6 min read · Updated 21 July 2026

A 1.5 ton AC run 8 hours a day adds roughly 250–370 units a month to your meter, depending on its star rating and age — and because slab tariffs are telescopic, every one of those units is billed at your highest applicable rate, not the average. This guide shows the actual math: units per hour by star rating, what that costs at typical marginal rates, and what genuinely reduces it.

How many units does a 1.5 ton AC use per hour?

"1.5 ton" describes cooling output (about 5,100 watts of heat removed), not electricity drawn. What the meter records depends on the AC's efficiency — its ISEER (Indian Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio), which is what the BEE star label grades. The seasonal average is simply:

Units per hour ≈ cooling watts ÷ ISEER ÷ 1000

1.5 ton AC typeTypical ISEERUnits per hour (avg)8 h/day × 30 days
5-star inverter (current label)~5.0+≈ 1.0≈ 245 units
3-star inverter~3.9–4.0≈ 1.3≈ 310 units
Old fixed-speed / pre-2022 label~3.0–3.3≈ 1.5–1.7≈ 370–410 units

These are seasonal averages: an inverter AC draws more in the first pull-down minutes and much less once the room holds temperature, so a well-insulated room at a moderate setting can beat these numbers, while a west-facing room at 18 °C will exceed them.

The slab trap: AC units are billed at your highest rate

This is the part most cost estimates get wrong. Domestic tariffs in most states are telescopic slabs — the first units are cheap, later units cost progressively more. Your fridge, fans and lights already fill the cheap slabs; the AC's 250–400 units stack on top, landing in the most expensive slabs you touch.

So the honest cost of running an AC is its units × your marginal rate — the rate of the last slab your total consumption reaches — plus the fuel surcharge (FPPA) and electricity duty that most states apply on top. In many states, adding an AC also pushes your total consumption past a slab boundary, silently re-pricing part of your existing usage too (and in states with non-telescopic or subsidy-threshold tariffs, crossing the limit can re-rate the entire bill).

What that costs per month

Marginal domestic rates in India today mostly fall between ₹5 and ₹12 per unit once you are past the lifeline slabs. Using the table above:

Five things that actually cut the AC bill

  • Set 24–26 °C, not 18–20 °C. Each degree cooler adds roughly 6% to consumption. 24 °C with a ceiling fan feels like 22 °C and saves 20–30% versus an 18 °C habit — the single biggest free saving.
  • Service before summer. Choked filters and dirty condenser coils force longer compressor runs; a ₹500 service routinely saves 10–15%.
  • Seal the room. Door gaps, single-pane west windows and uninsulated ceilings are why "the AC runs all night." Curtains and draft strips are cheaper than units.
  • Use timer/sleep mode. A room holds its chill; running 5 hours with a sleep curve instead of 8 flat hours cuts a third of the units.
  • Do the 5-star math before replacing. Upgrading an old fixed-speed AC (~1.6 units/hr) to a 5-star inverter (~1.0) saves ~145 units/month over an 8 h/day summer — ₹1,000+ per month at typical marginal rates, so heavy users recover the price difference in two or three seasons. Light users (2–3 h/day) may never recover it.

Check what an AC would do to your bill

Take last month's units, add the AC estimate for your star rating and hours from the table above, and run both numbers through the bill calculator for your state. The difference — computed on your DISCOM's real slabs, fixed charges, FPPA and duty — is the true monthly cost of the AC, including any slab boundary you cross on the way.

Frequently asked questions

How many units does a 1.5 ton AC consume in one hour?
Roughly 1 unit per hour for a current 5-star inverter model, about 1.3 units for a 3-star inverter, and 1.5–1.7 units for an old fixed-speed AC — seasonal averages computed as cooling watts (≈5,100 for 1.5 ton) divided by the ISEER on the BEE label.
What is the monthly electricity bill for a 1.5 ton AC running 8 hours a day?
Around 245–410 units a month depending on star rating and age. At marginal domestic rates of ₹6–10 per unit that is roughly ₹1,500–₹4,000 per month, plus fuel surcharge and electricity duty. The exact figure depends on your state slabs — the AC units are billed at your highest applicable slab rate.
Does an inverter AC really reduce the electricity bill?
Yes, for anyone running the AC several hours daily. An inverter compressor modulates instead of stop-starting, so a 5-star inverter averages ~1 unit/hour against ~1.6 for an old fixed-speed unit — about 35–40% fewer units for the same cooling.
What AC temperature setting saves the most electricity?
24–26 °C. Consumption rises roughly 6% for every degree below that, so 18 °C costs about a third more than 24 °C. Pairing 24–25 °C with a ceiling fan gives the same comfort as a much colder setting.
Why did my bill double after buying an AC?
Two effects stack: the AC adds 250–400 units, and those units land in your highest tariff slabs — in some states they also push you past a subsidy or slab threshold that re-prices existing usage. Compare your pre-AC and post-AC units in a bill calculator to separate the two effects.
