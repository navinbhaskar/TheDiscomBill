Electricity Subsidy Eligibility Checker

Government electricity subsidies are real money left on the table — but the schemes are scattered across central and state portals and almost impossible to compare. Tell us who you are and where you live, and we'll check the three that matter: the PM Surya Ghar rooftop-solar subsidy (up to ₹78,000), your state's free-units / lifeline scheme, and farm-power eligibility — with an estimated benefit for your usage.

How PM Surya Ghar rooftop-solar subsidy works

Under the PM Surya Ghar: Muft Bijli Yojana, the central government pays a fixed subsidy on a residential rooftop solar system: ₹30,000 per kW for the first 2 kW and ₹18,000 for the 3rd kW, capped at ₹78,000 for a 3 kW or larger system. Many states add a top-up on top of this. You apply on the national portal, pick an empanelled vendor, and the subsidy lands in your bank account after installation and net-meter commissioning. Estimate the payback on the rest with our rooftop solar calculator.

Free units, lifeline slabs and farm power

Separately from solar, many states waive the energy charge on a block of free units every month for domestic consumers, or price a low-consumption lifeline slab cheaply. Agricultural pump-set connections are supplied free or at a heavily subsidised flat rate in most states. These are state schemes, so the caps and rules change with each tariff order — the checker applies your state's current modelled scheme and links you to verify. See the bill-increase guide if a subsidy silently dropped off your bill.

Estimates use each state's published domestic tariff and the current PM Surya Ghar central subsidy slab; state top-ups, agricultural tariffs and lifeline rules vary by DISCOM and change with every tariff order. This is a planning guide — confirm eligibility and current amounts on the official PM Surya Ghar portal and your DISCOM's tariff schedule before applying.
